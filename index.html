Dr. (Mrs.) Maryam I.B. Babangida

Visionary Founder (1995)

In September 1995, Dr. (Mrs.) Maryam Babangida, former First Lady of Nigeria, established El-Amin International School in Minna with a revolutionary vision: to create an institution that would transform Nigerian education through excellence, values, and innovation.

"Her leadership emphasized moral discipline as the foundation, creativity as the vehicle, and leadership as the destination for every student's journey."

Beginning with the primary precursor in the early 1990s and expanding to secondary education in 1995, her unwavering commitment saw El-Amin grow into the premier institution it is today.

Expansion & Continuation

Abuja Campus

On October 2, 2002, Hajiya Jummai Sambo expanded the family's educational vision by opening the Abuja branch in Maitama, bringing El-Amin's transformative education to the nation's capital.

Family Legacy

The school continues under the guidance of Dr. Muhammad Babangida as Executive Director, ensuring the founder's vision remains alive and vibrant across both campuses.

Steady Growth

From humble beginnings with just 76 pupils at the Abuja campus, El-Amin has grown to serve hundreds of students across Nursery, Primary, and Secondary education.
